The Problem: A $33B Opportunity for Investors

For investors, the problem of the cannabis industry’s inability to secure compliant payment solutions presents a major opportunity. With a $33 billion industry largely limited to cash-only transactions, the demand for a reliable, legal, and transparent payment service is immense. This is where the investor in Tangi token stands to benefit. As the only compliant payment solution with a built-in token incentive, we are positioned to capitalize on this $33 billion market gap. While other providers have failed to deliver, Tangi's token provides a solution that meets the needs of both retailers and consumers, ensuring massive adoption. As we fill this gap, the value of the token grows, directly benefiting investors who got in early and positioned themselves for long-term success. Here’s how we make that happen.

Despite the legal cannabis industry being a $33 billion cash cow, major banks and card networks like Visa and MasterCard refuse to service the industry, limiting retailers to accepting cash only payments. This has left 8,000 retailers and 60 million consumers—in a cash-only system.


A typical dispensary handles up to $250,000 per week in cash, making it a target for armed robbery and needing armoured trucks to transport large volumes of money. Cash-only transactions also limit customer spending, complicate accounting, and hinder access to loans and other financial support.

Meanwhile, 60 million consumers are forced to carry cash and are excluded from loyalty programs, which are standard in every other retail industry, as there is no way of tracking their spending habits.

The inability to secure a banking partner is the single biggest reason why payment service providers cannot deliver legitimate payment solutions in this space, and it has created an environment where illegal, non-compliant providers exploit retailers with unreliable services and exorbitant fees.

Without a banking partner, there’s no way for payment providers to deposit funds processed from cannabis sales. As a result, retailers are stuck relying mainly on cash transactions or worse, illegal and non-compliant payment providers who exploit the retailers desperate need for an alternative to cash payments.

Most of those non-compliant providers have come and gone, and those managing to go undetected are operating under the radar. It’s only a matter of time before they face the same fate. The industry is in desperate need of a compliant, reliable, and sustainable solution, and every other provider has failed to deliver it.

As one of our struggling competitors aptly put it, 'It's like trying to run a marathon in cement shoes,' says Sarah Johnson, CEO of CannaPay Solutions, a third-party payment provider. 'We have the technology and the demand, but without banking partners, we're stuck in a financial no-man's-land.”

This is the reality the cannabis industry has been facing—until now.
